California's Power Distribution

The movement of electric power across a given geographic area, is coordinated by the regional system operator -- affectionately dubbed "the grid". In California, the grid is called CAISO, pronounced Cal-ISO. (Why? Why not?)

Many electric grids are also open markets, with the trading of power positions. Utilities purchase electricity in order to supply their customers, and power plants sell their power openly on the market. The job of CAISO is to make sure that everybody plays by the rules, and to make sure that the "lights stay on" no matter what the market may bring.

To the left is a display showing the current % of fuel mix used in CAISO to power all our homes and business. This power is coordinated by the CAISO every 5 minutes, and the ratios to the left will also updated every 5 mintues to reflect this change.
